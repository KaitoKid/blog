Back for the Summer - 2018 Edition

Posted on 2018-05-21

And I’m Back

Honestly, I don’t even know if this counts as being back. My previous blog post was just about a year ago where I just learned about how to setup and use Hexo. To be completely honest, I actually lost the files for that blog - I had to copy-paste everything from the static website over and regenerate the files from markdown.

In the end, I even removed the second how-to post because it wasn’t really relevant. Part 3 was supposed to be about how to get HTTPS on our own domain with GitHub pages, but now that GitHub has added that functionality… there’s really no point for part 3 either.

So yea, here I am. Essentially with a fresh start. Hopefully I’ll be able to be more consistent this time around with this blog and see where I can go. I’m expecting myself to go pretty ham for the first couple of days or weeks, and eventually slow it down to maybe one post a month.

Why I’m Blogging

I’ve been considering about it for a while, and it’s just something I’ve considered since middle school when a relatively close friend, Chelsey, used to blog. I say relatively because I didn’t know her super well back then, but now she’s one of my bestest friends. If you’re reading this Chelsey, thanks <3

Over the years, I’ve started blogs up, taken them down, and all that jazz but I’ve never actually dedicated myself to it - or seen the need to. It’s just like that one small project where in the beginning you’re super excited about it, but as time goes on, you realize how truly lazy you are and forget about it.

This time around though, it might be different. I’m mainly using this as an outlet as like a diary or a scrapbook, just a lot more messy and uncoordinated. I don’t intend to SEO, monetize, or even promote this blog. I probably won’t even list it in any of my social media profiles - I kinda just ended up on this domain because it’s where my Google account/email is grandfathered. I’ll be sharing my portfolio if anyone asks for my ‘website’.

Closing Statements

To close it off for this post, I’m just going to talk about what’s been going on with my life. I guess it’s a lot longer than a closing should be, but I’m edgy like that so let’s kick it off with….

Starting at Workday

Today was my first official day as an App Dev intern at WorkDay. I’m currently staying in these apartments about 5 miles away from campus in a single due to insomnia issues. The distance is killer though, my best plan so far is to get a Boosted Board, bus to as close as I can get, and then skateboard the rest of the way. I’ve never skateboarded before, so hopefully this works out. My housemate, Gayashan Ediriweera, is absolutely incredible. He’s super chill and is married. This guy used to be a Senior Consultant at Deloitte and went back to ‘Vandy’ to do his MBA. He even was featured on his university’s website, what a legend.

I honestly feel kind of bad because when I moved in, I picked the Master Bedroom since it was the larger room - then I had to move into the smaller one because G’s got his wife coming over later over the summer. Oops, sorry man.

The intern orientation was honestly about as boring as it gets. We just covered the company’s core values etc. I also ended up with the wrong laptop style - a MacBook Air instead of a MacBook Pro. I feel like coding and running VMs on an Air just gunna result in a lot of headaches, gotta go back in tomorrow to swap it out. I also need to go back home and grab my passport for the I-9 paperwork ughhhhh…

First thing I did when I got into the company Slack was join the #fortnite-br, #overwatch, and #gaming channels. Gamers will be gamers LOL

My Health Problems

To be fair, I’m not sure how many of these are real problems and how many of them are just due to my poor lifestyle and life choices. It just sucks because I’m one of those people that get anxiety over it, and my brain just accentuates the pain/discomfort.

Carpal Tunnel

Or at least I think it’s carpal tunnel. My left hand and forearm are just strugglin’ and will have moments where they do feel tinges of numbness. I really do need to wash my brace and start wearing it again at night - I feel like it did help back when I used to wear it.

Anxiety

Ooooh boy, this is a big one. I started having anxiety sometime back in Oct/Nov of 2017 during school where I fell pretty ill and never truly recovered. I got nausea, dizziness, general body fatigue, and felt like shit all day. I spent many hundreds of dollars and months of my time going to doctors trying to figure out what was wrong. During that time, I fell to the lowest of the lows: depression. I barely made it through, and every day was such a struggle. I went through counseling, physical therapy, and a huge range of rehab until around February 2018. I told myself that I’ve had enough, and would just live my life even if I would be ‘disabled’ for the rest of my life.

Starting them, I saw things just get better over time. However, after my birthday trip to Toronto where I got too high off of an edible, I developed severe anxiety again - alongside insomnia among other things. I’m working on it, and really hoping that I’ll make it through again this time around!

Vision

My vision has degraded at an alarming rate since I stopped wearing night time hard contacts, and I really do need to schedule one last follow-up with UCB Eye Center. God I love those folks.

Dental

Ok, I’m 99% sure I have a cavity cus when I thought I was dying, hygiene was the last thing on my mind. Going to a dentist tomorrow and I’m absolutely terrified. I also lost my retainer, but I got a new one from a terrific orthodontist. Shit hurts though ugh.

Rash

Not gunna go into too much details about this one… sucks being hairy -_-

That’s It?

I think that’s it this time around. I can’t think of any other stuff to rant or talk about. I lied, there’s plenty, like my experience at Microsoft Build, my goals for this summer, Maplestory 2, and probably a billion other more that I just haven’t written down yet. But once again, this blog is more for me to gather my thoughts and relieve some of all that pent of stress, frustration, anxiety, yada-yada.

Hopefully I can keep this up!

What’s Hexo?

Hexo is a static website generator that runs on NodeJS. It has a large community behind the project is a popular choice for blogs and personal websites hosted on GitHub pages. Unlike Jekyll, a similar generator, Hexo supports plugins in its themes.

Unlike other competitors like Wordpress, Ghost, and Medium, Hexo allows you to easily modify the HTML and Javascript. This flexibility was what prompted me to eventually choose Hexo myself.
